How the Bot Works (1 minute read)

The bot watches the Aviator crash history and waits for a trigger condition — a sign that the next round might have a higher multiplier. When triggered, it places bets on both panels for up to N rounds, then goes back to watching.

  1. Watch mode — the bot reads every crash result but places no bets. It's looking for your trigger condition.
  2. Trigger fires — when it sees the pattern you set (a high crash, or a long streak of low crashes), it activates betting mode.
  3. Bet mode — it bets on both panels for up to Max Bet Rounds rounds. Panel 1 auto-cashes out at your P1 multiplier; Panel 2 at your P2 multiplier.
  4. Win or exhaust — if a round wins, it resets and goes back to watching. If all rounds are used without a win, it takes the loss and watches again.
  5. Recovery — if recovery is ON, Panel 1's bet size scales up each round to recover previous losses. When recovery is OFF, it bets the same flat amount every time.
Key insight

The strategy is about when to bet, not trying to predict every round. A trigger is just a signal that conditions look favourable — it's never a guarantee.

Basic Parameters

Trigger Mode
Basic
Both — activates when a high crash OR a low streak is seen.
High crash only — only fires after a crash above your Trigger multiplier. Good for sniping big-multiplier follow-ups.
Low streak only — only fires after many consecutive low crashes. Good for timing a recovery after a bad run.
Recommended start: Both
High Crash Trigger (×)
Basic
After a crash above this multiplier the bot starts betting. The logic: a big crash just happened — the next few rounds may also fly higher than average.
Lower value → triggers more often (more bets, more exposure).
Higher value → triggers rarely (fewer bets, waits for big events).
Default: 9× — only fires after a rare 9× or higher crash
Low Streak Max (×) & Window (rounds)
Basic
If the last Window rounds all crashed at or below Low Streak Max, the bot fires. The logic: a long run of tiny crashes makes a higher one more likely.
Smaller Max → only fires after a very bad streak.
Larger Window → requires more rounds of lows before triggering (more confident signal).
Default: ≤3× for 8 rounds
Panel 1 Cashout (×)
Basic
Panel 1 auto-cashes out at this multiplier. This is your main recovery panel — when it wins, the recovery deficit resets to zero.
Higher = bigger payout per win, but the crash needs to fly further. Lower = wins more often but pays less per win.
Default: 6×
Panel 2 Cashout (×)
Basic
Panel 2 always bets a flat Bet Amount KES and cashes out here. It's a smaller hedge — it wins more frequently than Panel 1 and softens losses.
Keep this lower than Panel 1.
Default: 3×
Stop on Profit / Stop on Loss (KES)
Basic
Global session guards. The bot stops completely when your total P&L for the session hits either of these limits — regardless of what the trigger is doing.
Stop on Profit: take your winnings and walk away.
Stop on Loss: cut losses before they grow. Must be a negative number.
Default: +500 KES profit / −200 KES loss

Advanced Parameters

These control how the bot bets once triggered. Safe to leave at defaults until you understand the basics.

Recovery / Martingale
Advanced
ON — after a losing round, Panel 1's bet scales up to recover all previous losses plus a small profit target. This means one win can wipe the entire deficit.
OFF — Panel 1 always bets the flat Bet Amount. Losses don't compound, but recovery is slower.
Default: ON
Recovery Target (KES)
Advanced
When recovery is ON, this is the profit margin added on top of the deficit before calculating Panel 1's bet. A higher target means a bigger win when P1 hits, but also a bigger bet.
P1 bet = (deficit + target) ÷ P1 cashout
e.g. deficit=16, target=5, P1=6× → bet = 21÷6 = 3.50 KES
Default: 5 KES
Bet Amount (KES)
Advanced
The base bet for both panels. Panel 2 always uses exactly this amount. Panel 1 starts at this amount and scales up if recovery is ON.
Default: 1 KES (SportPesa minimum)
Max Bet Rounds
Advanced
How many rounds the bot keeps betting after a trigger, before giving up and returning to watch mode. Fewer rounds = lower max exposure. More rounds = more chances to recover.
Default: 4 rounds
Burst Cooldown (rounds)
Advanced
After a betting burst ends (win or rounds used up), the bot skips this many watch rounds before accepting the next trigger. Prevents rapid back-to-back triggering during a volatile patch.
Set to 0 to disable.
Default: 0 (no cooldown)
Stop after N consecutive losses
Advanced
If Panel 1 loses this many rounds in a row (across any number of bursts), the session stops entirely. A safety net for when the market is not cooperating.
Set to 0 to disable.
Default: 0 (disabled)

How to Build a Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Decide your risk appetite first.
    Low risk: small bets, tight stop-loss, fewer rounds. High risk: bigger bets, wider guards, more rounds. Everything else follows from this.
  2. Pick a trigger mode.
    New to the bot? Use Both. Once you've watched a few sessions, switch to the trigger that fired most usefully for the time of day you play.
  3. Set cashouts relative to your trigger.
    If you trigger on high crashes (9×+), your Panel 1 cashout should be moderate (6×–8×) — you expect some upward momentum but not necessarily another mega crash. If you trigger on low streaks, a lower P1 cashout (3×–5×) hits more reliably.
  4. Set stop guards before you start.
    A common rule: Stop on Loss = 40% of your session bankroll. If you're playing with 500 KES, set −200. Stop on Profit at 2–3× your stop-loss.
  5. Decide on recovery.
    Recovery ON is more aggressive — one P1 win clears all losses. Recovery OFF is safer but you need more wins to break even. If your bankroll is small, turn recovery OFF.
  6. Add a burst cooldown if you see back-to-back triggers.
    If the bot triggers 3 times in 10 rounds and keeps losing, set a cooldown of 3–5 rounds to let the market settle.
  7. Start with a preset, tweak one value at a time.
    Copy "Conservative" or "Default" and change only one parameter. Watch the session. Adjust. Never change 3 things at once — you won't know what worked.
Important

No strategy guarantees profit. Aviator is a provably fair random game. The bot automates a disciplined approach — it cannot predict the crash multiplier. Always set a stop-loss you can afford to lose.
